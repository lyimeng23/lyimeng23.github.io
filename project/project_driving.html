About the Project

Our project is dedicated to enhancing driving safety for older adults, especially those with minor cognitive impairments. We are developing advanced in-car monitoring systems combined with realistic simulation testing and community education. Our goal is to identify early signs of cognitive decline that may affect driving ability and to provide interventions that preserve independence while ensuring safety.

Project Goals

  • Assess Driving Capability: Develop simulation protocols to evaluate driving performance in older adults, focusing on cognitive challenges.
  • Innovative Monitoring: Create an in-car sensor system to continuously monitor driving behavior and detect early signs of risky driving.
  • Community Education: Provide accessible workshops and resources to educate seniors and their families about cognitive health and driving safety.

Why It Matters

Safe driving is key to maintaining independence for older adults. However, even minor cognitive changes can increase driving risks. By detecting these changes early, our project helps prevent accidents and supports timely interventions. Our combined approach of technology and community outreach promotes informed decision-making, ensuring safer roads for everyone.

Project Timeline & Future Work

  • Months 1-3: Establish a simulation lab and develop testing protocols with stakeholder feedback.
  • Months 3-7: Conduct simulation tests and analyze driving behavior data across different participant groups.
  • Months 7-10: Develop and pilot-test the in-vehicle monitoring prototype in real-world driving conditions.
  • Months 8-12: Implement community education workshops and collect feedback to refine our curriculum.
  • Future Work: Refine tools based on testing, expand real-world trials, scale up educational initiatives, and adapt technology for broader applications.
